Puerto Rico

The slow road to storm recovery

By Peyton Chance

On this date, Hurricane Maria battered the island of Puerto Rico with winds up to this miles per hour. The storm destroyed the homes of hundreds of thousands of Puerto Ricans. The road to recovery has been marked with challenges and understanding these obstacles is only made more complicated by the varied coverage of the disaster by American news outlets.

Power in Puerto Rico: What we know

The storm left Puerto Ricans with incredibly limited access to electric power. Hurricane Maria made landfall on the island on September 20, 2017. On September 29, more than a week after the storm hit, the Puerto Rico Electric Power Authority reported that just 5% of the island had power.

Over the last two months, the pace of recovery for the Puerto Rican power system has been glacial. The graph below displays the slow increase in power access through October and November. To this day, more than 35% of the island is still without power.

Americans left in the dark

As Puerto Rico is a U.S. territory, its residence are United States citizens. Puerto Rico is closer to Washington, D.C. than Los Angeles, yet its residents have received minimal assistance in restoring power.
